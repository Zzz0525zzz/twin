## 2. Results and Discussion draft

Ref. and 90┬░ DW reveal how an initial domain structure changes the mechanical pathway under an otherwise identical compression path. The two configurations share the same BaTiO3 model, cell geometry, and y-axis loading protocol, with the initial domain structure as the controlled distinction (Fig. 1). Under this common compression path, Ref. reaches the larger compressive-stress magnitude over much of the loading range, whereas 90┬░ DW carries the imposed strain at a lower magnitude and shows an oscillatory stress evolution through the early-to-intermediate loading stage (Fig. 2a). The 90┬░ DW response therefore changes both the stress level and the way stress evolves during loading, consistent with a non-smooth strain-accommodation process. Because polarization redistribution provides a natural route for strain accommodation in ferroelectrics, this mechanical contrast should be reflected in the evolution of the loading-axis polarization.

The averaged polarization curves show that the two stress histories are accompanied by different timing of loading-axis polarization redistribution. In Ref., the early Py remains small despite a large |Py|, showing that loading-axis components are present locally but cancel in the signed average. As compression proceeds, Ref. develops a delayed increase in signed Py before both Py and |Py| are suppressed, indicating a redistribution of local loading-axis components rather than a simple monotonic loss from the initial state. In 90┬░ DW, Py and |Py| are initially close, so the loading-axis component is more sign-coherent before it decreases during compression. At larger strain, the signed Py oscillates around a small magnitude while |Py| varies more smoothly, indicating residual sign redistribution after most Py strength has been reduced. The averaged curves therefore establish polarization rearrangement, but they do not resolve the spatial pathway through which local Py is removed.

The local Py maps identify the spatial route of loading-axis polarization removal. The three marked states in Fig. 3, ╬Ąy = ŌłÆ1.0%, ŌłÆ1.5%, and ŌłÆ2.0%, sample the onset, intermediate development, and near-completion of loading-axis Py suppression. In Ref., local Py is still retained in part of the cell at the first marked state and then weakens through a nonuniform reorientation away from the loading axis. In 90┬░ DW, the evolving Py field retains the imprint of the initial 90┬░ domain-wall pattern even as the Py magnitude is strongly reduced, with local polarization reorienting around that pattern. The important difference is not the eventual suppression of Py, but the spatial route by which that suppression occurs. The initial domain structure therefore selects a polarization redistribution pathway during compression, providing the spatial basis for the different mechanical responses.

The pathway difference in the local Py field requires a scalar measure of loading-axis polarization strength. A signed average removes oppositely oriented local components, although those components still contribute to the local loading-axis polarization strength. We therefore use the normalized second moment

m_y(e) = <P_y┬▓>_e / <P_y┬▓>_0,

where the brackets denote spatial averaging. Unlike <Py>, m_y retains this local contribution and tracks the remaining loading-axis Py strength during compression. Figure 4a shows that Ref. and 90┬░ DW initially follow similar m_y trajectories, but separate after the first marked stage. Ref. retains an intermediate shoulder in m_y, whereas 90┬░ DW continues toward lower values, consistent with earlier removal of loading-axis Py strength along the domain-wall-organized pathway. Both structures eventually approach a low-m_y state; a final-state comparison alone would therefore miss the distinct timing and trajectories of Py-strength loss.

The Py-strength trajectory provides the polarization variable needed to write the stress response in a reduced form. We use

s(e) Ōēł a_e e + a_m[1 ŌłÆ m_y(e)],

where a_e e gives the smooth strain-driven background and 1 ŌłÆ m_y(e) measures the progress of loading-axis Py-strength loss. In Fig. 4b, this expression places the higher-stress Ref. branch and the lower, non-smooth 90┬░ DW branch on their corresponding polarization-strength trajectories, rather than treating both responses as strain-only curves. The strain-only reference in the Supplementary Information retains the smooth background but does not reproduce the structure-sensitive variation associated with 90┬░ DW. The common-coefficient form further shows that the role of the polarization-strength term does not come only from assigning separate coefficients to the two structures. The reduced relation therefore connects the stress response to the domain-selected pathway of Py-strength redistribution.

By linking the local Py-redistribution route to a stress-relevant trajectory, the reduced relation shows that Ref. and 90┬░ DW differ in load accommodation rather than only in stress magnitude. The mechanical response is shaped by the imposed compression and by the route through which the initial domain structure removes loading-axis Py strength. Domain-structure control can therefore tune how ferroelectrics carry mechanical load, not only by setting the initial polarization state but also by selecting the polarization-mediated stress-evolution pathway.

# Writing and Model-Workflow Guardrails for This Letter

These rules should guide all subsequent manuscript edits. They are not manuscript text; they are working constraints for drafting, revision, and model-assisted editing.

## A. Target-journal writing principles

1. Treat the manuscript as a Letter, not a full experimental report. Every paragraph must advance one central result: domain structure changes the polarization pathway, and that pathway is expressed in the mechanical response.
2. Prioritize mechanism over chronology. Do not simply explain Fig. 1, then Fig. 2, then Fig. 3, then Fig. 4. Figures may appear in order, but the prose must be driven by the scientific problem.
3. Keep the Scripta Materialia target in mind: short, direct, structure-property oriented, and mechanistic. The text should emphasize how an initial structural state changes a functional/mechanical response through a specific pathway.
4. Keep the APL target in mind as a secondary style: concise, physics-facing, and centered on one clear applied-physics result. Avoid long methodological justification in the main Results.
5. Figure 2 introduces the mechanical contrast; Figure 3 gives spatial evidence for different local Py pathways; Figure 4 is the quantitative core that connects polarization-strength evolution to the dominant stress response.
6. The Results should not read as a balanced description of all figures. It should make the strongest result visible: the 90┬░ DW stress response, especially its early oscillatory features, is tied to the polarization-strength pathway and is not recovered from strain alone.

## B. Anti-AI writing rules

1. Do not use generic summary phrases such as ŌĆ£central conclusion,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£taken together,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£mechanism-level link,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£trend-level closure,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£compact field-level signature,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£the missing information is,ŌĆØ or ŌĆ£this controlled comparison allows.ŌĆØ
2. Avoid paragraph templates of the form ŌĆ£A shows X; however B cannot explain Y; therefore C provides Z.ŌĆØ This is logically clear but reads like AI-generated explanation.
3. Prefer concrete subjects over abstract subjects. Use ŌĆ£90┬░ DW,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£Ref.,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£the stress oscillations,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£m_y,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£the strain-only baseline,ŌĆØ and ŌĆ£the local Py fieldŌĆØ as subjects whenever possible.
4. Avoid process-reporting verbs unless necessary: ŌĆ£we examine,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£we compare,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£we convert,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£we test.ŌĆØ Prefer result-driven verbs: ŌĆ£separates,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£decreases,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£follows,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£misses,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£retains,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£organizes,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£accounts for.ŌĆØ

6. Do not polish AI-like paragraphs after the fact only by replacing words. AI flavor often comes from structure, subject choice, and claim ordering. Each paragraph must be shaped correctly before final polishing.
7. Avoid unsupported elegance. A sentence is only good if it points to a visible feature in the figure or a specific formula/model comparison.

## C. Sentence-level quality checks

Before accepting any sentence, check:

1. Does the sentence contain a concrete object from the study, or only abstract nouns?
2. Does it point to a visible result, a measured trend, or a defined quantity?
3. Does it make one claim only?
4. Is the claim stronger than the data allow?
5. Would the sentence still matter if the figure number were removed?
6. Does the sentence sound like an author interpreting data, or like an assistant explaining a workflow?
7. Can the sentence be shortened without losing the scientific claim?

## D. Results drafting workflow

Use this workflow instead of one-step full-section rewriting.

1. For each paragraph, first write the Chinese author judgment: what scientific point must this paragraph establish?
2. List the figure facts that must support that judgment.
3. Draft 2ŌĆō3 English versions with different styles: short Scripta-style, slightly explanatory APL-style, and a more complete backup version.
4. Select sentences, not whole AI-generated paragraphs.
5. Integrate selected sentences into the canvas only after checking claim strength, figure support, and language tone.
6. After all paragraphs are stable, perform final polish for concision, terminology, mathematical notation, and flow.
